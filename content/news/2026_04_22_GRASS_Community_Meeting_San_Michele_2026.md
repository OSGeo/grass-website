# Join us for the GRASS Community Meeting 2026 in San Michele all'Adige!

🚆 **Arrival:** Saturday, July 11, 2026<br>
🚆 **Departure:** Sunday, July 19, 2026<br>
📍 **Location:** Fondazione Edmund Mach (FEM), San Michele all'Adige, Trentino, Italy

We're excited to announce the **GRASS Community Meeting 2026**,
the main annual gathering of the GRASS community!

## Why come

A week with the rest of the team **in one place and one time zone** is a rare
chance to finish the maintenance, infrastructure, and large changes that stall
in async review. A quick "come look at this" saves days of back and forth.
This year's focus: the **Python API transition** and **finishing the CMake
build system transition**. Around that core, there's plenty of room to improve
processing tools, documentation, and the interfaces to QGIS, GDAL, PostGIS, R,
OGC services, and others. That rewrite you've been meaning to get to but
wanted community input for? This is the time!

The meeting is open to all users, supporters, contributors, power users,
and developers *who want to make a meaningful contribution to the
project*. The
[2025 event report](https://grass.osgeo.org/news/2025_05_27_grass_dev_summit_report/)
gives a sense of what a week like this can achieve.

## Why San Michele

The meeting is hosted at [Fondazione Edmund Mach](https://fmach.it/) (FEM),
a research and education center about 15 km north of Trento. On-site
facilities let participants **work, sleep, and eat in the same location**,
keeping the community together for the full week. Trento is also the host
city of [FOSS4G Italy 2026](https://2026.foss4g.it/) (July 9–11), making it
easy to combine both events. Also, **the place is absolutely gorgeous**, with
vineyards and the foothills of Dolomites just beyond the campus.

## Sign up and stay tuned

Participation is **free of charge**. Add your name and find logistics,
venue details, and the agenda on the
[wiki page](https://grasswiki.osgeo.org/wiki/GRASS_Community_Meeting_San_Michele_2026).

## Support the event

Funding directly shapes how many people can take part. Recent meetings drew
**16–17 in-person participants** in 2023 and 2024, and **34 in 2025** thanks
to additional funding that covered travel for students and contributors from
far away. Your support grows the room. The fastest way to help is a direct
donation through [NumFOCUS](https://numfocus.org/donate-to-grass); for more
options, such as invoicing or corporate sponsorship, see the
[Sponsoring page](/contribute/sponsoring/).
